Art of Statistics

"Anyone can make an estimate," but you need statistics to quantify precision.

Now it's important to be more precise about:
  • population parameters, like μ and σ
  • sample statistics, like m and s
In EDS Notebook 11, we compute the sample mean, m, and use it to estimate the population mean, μ.

Then we use resampling to compute a confidence interval, which is also a sample statistic.  And we can ask "how likely is it that my CI contains μ?

One vocabulary note:  Resampling is a general term for randomized statistical method, also known as Monte Carlo methods.  

Resampling methods include:
  • Bootstrapping, which is the more specific name for drawing a sample from a sample (and jackknifing, which is similar).
  • Permutation tests, which are based on shuffling data.  We'll see an example next week.
Spiegelhalter acknowledges that resampling is computationally-intensive.

He says it's not practical for large sample sizes, but even with n=106, it's probably fast enough.
