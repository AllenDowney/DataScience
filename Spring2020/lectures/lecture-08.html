Art of Statistics Chapter 4

Intention to treat

If you assign a patient to Treatment A but they actually follow Treatment B, how should you count them?

Usually, you do intention-to-treat analysis

Why?  From the POV of public health, you have control of assignment but not adherence.

You want to know how effective it is to ASSIGN Treatment A.  If adherence is low, that's part of effectiveness.

Consider two treatments for obesity, a miserable diet and a pill with serious side effects.

The diet might be better for patients who adhere to it, but if adherence is low, the pill might be better, on average, despite the side effects.

Why might ITT be the wrong thing to do?

In both cases:

2) Within most departments, admission rate for women is the same or higher.

3) More women applied to low-rate departments.
