Draft reports

If you gave me a draft, I made comments.  Be aware that I save time by not bothering with the compliment sandwich :)

If not, give me something and I will turn it around as fast as I can.

If you want to talk in person, we can do that.

In the drafts I read, the analysis is generally solid.  You are finding interesting things!

The most common elements that need work are:

1) Cut the things the reader doesn't need.  Don't transcribe the notebook; find the story and tell it.

2) Don't forget QMRI.  I'm seeing good QM, but then missing pieces of RI.

3) Write in the reader's language.  For example, readers are interested in responses to questions, not variables.

4) As always, the first and last paragraphs are the hardest.  Write them last.  Don't overpromise.  Don't overgeneralize.  Do start with context and end with context.
