Verb tense

We have three competing mental time lines:

1) The progress of your project (First we did this, then we did that)

2) The progress of the reader through the report.  (We will tell you... We told you.)

3) The 50 years of the GSS survey.

We want to talk about (3).  The others get in the way.

Open Data Competition

The Boston/Cambridge PyData Meetup is running an Open Data Competition

Here are some of the datasets they suggest working with

If you start a project for the PyData, you could finish it for this class.

As I've said before, when you are the first person to explore datasets like this, it is very likely that you can find interesting, important things with simple exploratory tools.

A few years ago there was a major news story based on exploration of this dataset.

A successful data science project combines:

1) A question that matters

2) A dataset that can answer it

3) Methods that connect the dataset to the question

If you can get these things to go together, you can have impact.

But where should you start?

Q: Maybe it's unanswerable

D: Maybe it's uninteresting

M: Maybe it's inapplicable

For the project, I suggest starting with a rich dataset that is reasonably available.

Note: A dataset that takes some work to bring online might actually be a good thing.

You will almost certainly want a raw dataset, rather than summary statistics someone else has computed.
