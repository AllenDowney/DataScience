Suppose the correlation coefficient between an independent variable and a dependent variable is close to 0.  Which of the following conclusions can you draw (with reasonable confidence)?
  1. there is a non-linear relationship between the variables
  2. if there is a linear relationship between them, it is weak
  3. there is no relationship between the variables

Summary statistics always lose information; the objective is to capture the important information.

Mean and standard deviation are vulnerable to extreme values.

Median and IQR are more "robust", often better for skewed distributions.

Income and wealth are famously skewed; reporting means is seldom a good choice.

Median is a little better; reporting several quantiles is even better.

QMRI

QMRI is a framework for thinking about data science writing and technical communication more generally.

Question: What is the motivation?  What do we want to know?

Methodology: How are we going about answering it? Describe the data.

Results: What is the output of the methodology, and how do we decode it?

Interpretation: How do we interpret the results as an answer to the motivating question? Discuss limitations.

Most good technical writing fits into this framework; or maybe it would be more precise to say that any technical writing that does not include these elements is probably not good.

1) No motivating question, or not presented clearly?  You've lost the reader before you started.

2) No methodology?  You've lost the power to persuade.

3) No decoding of the results?  You are asking the reader to do too much work, and you will lose most of them.

4) No interpretation?  The reader leaves wondering what the point was.

We will practice using this framework to compose, but first let's do some reverse engineering.
